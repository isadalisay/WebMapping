The Palisades Fire originated in the rugged, steep terrain of Topanga State Park, specifically within the Santa Monica Mountains near the community of Pacific Palisades. While the area is known for its natural beauty, the thick, dry brush and difficult-to-access canyons provided ample fuel for the flames to spread rapidly toward the coast.

Map 2:

The spread of the fire toward Malibu was dictated by the complex topography of the Santa Monica Mountains, which act as a natural wind tunnel for coastal air currents. As the flames moved west from the Pacific Palisades, they were funneled through steep, "V-shaped" canyons and over ridgelines where the acceleration of wind through narrow spaces pushed embers far ahead of the main fire front.

The Eaton Fire ignited on the evening of January 7, 2025, along the eastern hillside of Eaton Canyon Wash in the rugged foothills of the San Gabriel Mountains. The start of the fire was deeply tied to the local geography; it began near the intersection of Altadena Drive and Canyon Close Drive, an area where the steep, brush-heavy slopes of the Angeles National Forest meet the urban edge of Pasadena and Altadena.
